# King's Palace Museum (Rukari) - Visitor Guide

## About the Museum

The King's Palace Museum is located in Nyanza District, Southern Province, approximately 88 km from Kigali. It offers a detailed overview of Rwanda's monarchical system and its abolition in the early 1960s.

**Location:** Nyanza District, Southern Province, Rwanda
**Distance from Kigali:** 88 km
**Opening Hours:** Tuesday to Sunday, 7:00 AM - 6:00 PM
**Closed:** Mondays
**Opened as Museum:** May 2008

The museum consists of three main sections:
1. Traditional royal palace (replica)
2. Modern Art Deco palace
3. Inyambo cattle herd

---

## WHAT TO SEE

### 1. Traditional Royal Palace (Replica)

A beautifully crafted thatched dwelling shaped like a beehive, reconstructed to its 19th-century state using entirely traditional materials.

**Features:**
- Authentic architectural style
- Traditional construction methods
- Royal court layout
- Historical setting

**Historical Context:**
According to oral tradition, Nyanza was the heart of Rwanda and the site of battles and power struggles. The monarchy was mobile, moving the court between various locations. When it eventually settled, Nyanza became the obvious choice. The royal capital had as many as 2,000 inhabitants.

### 2. Modern Art Deco Palace

Built in 1932, this Art Deco style building served as the royal residence for King Mutara III Rudahigwa until his death in 1959.

**What to see inside:**
- Original furniture (some replicas due to loss in 1994)
- King's personal effects
- Family items
- Official duties artifacts
- Historical photographs
- Exhibition on monarchy from 15th to 20th century

**King Mutara III Rudahigwa:**
- Ruled from 1931-1959
- Converted to Christianity (becoming King Rudahigwa)
- Played key role in Rwanda's modern history
- His personal items are on display

### 3. Inyambo (Royal Cattle)

The museum is home to the famous long-horned Inyambo cattle - descendants of the royal herd.

**About Inyambo:**
- Known for their exceptionally long horns
- Historically kept by Rwandan royalty
- Symbol of wealth and status
- Still bred at the museum

---

## HISTORY OF RWANDAN MONARCHY

### The Kingdom of Rwanda

The monarchy in Rwanda dates back to the 15th century. The King (Mwami) held absolute power over the kingdom.

### Key Periods:
- **15th-19th Century:** Traditional monarchy
- **Late 19th Century:** Colonial influence begins
- **1931:** King Mutara III Rudahigwa comes to power
- **1959:** King Kigeri V (last king) overthrown
- **1962:** Rwanda becomes independent, monarchy abolished

### The Royal Court

The royal court was highly organized with:
- Royal advisors
- Ceremonial officials
- Traditional healers
- Guards and servants

- Court life documentation
- Pre-colonial Rwanda

## VISITOR INFORMATION

### Planning Your Visit

**Location:** Nyanza District, Southern Province
**Distance from Kigali:** 88 km (about 1.5 hours drive)
**Combined Ticket:** Available with Ethnographic Museum (Huye) and Rwesero Kwigira Museum

### What to Expect
1. **Traditional Palace:** Walk through the authentic replica
2. **Art Deco Palace:** Explore the modern royal residence
3. **Inyambo Cattle:** See the famous long-horned cows
4. **Guided Tours:** Available on site

### Recommended Time: 1-2 hours

### Tips for Visitors
- Wear comfortable shoes for walking
- Photography allowed in most areas
- Combine with Ethnographic Museum visit (30 km apart)
- Best visited in morning or late afternoon

## MULTILINGUAL SUMMARY (INGORO Y'ABAMI / PALAIS ROYAL)

### [RWANDA] Inyandiko mu Kinyarwanda (Amateka n'Ibiranga Ingoro y'Abami i Nyanza)
Ingoro y'Abami i Nyanza iherereye mu Karere ka Nyanza, mu Ntara y'Amajyepfo, ku birometero 88 uvuye i Kigali. Iyi ngoro igaragaza amateka y'ubwami bw'u Rwanda n'uburyo bwayoborwaga kugeza mu ntangiriro z'imyaka ya 1960.
- **Icyo wasura**: Inzu y'ibyatsi (beehive) yubatse mu buryo bwa gakondo, inzu ya Art Deco y'umwami Mutara III Rudahigwa, n'inyambo zifite amahembe maremare cyane.
- **Inyambo**: Ni inka z'umwami zirangwa n'amahembe maremare kandi akubye. Zimurikirwa abashyitsi nk'ikimenyetso cy'umuco.
- **Amasaha**: Ifunguye kuva kuwa Kabiri kugeza ku Cyumweru, kuva saa moya za mu gitondo kugeza saa kumi n'ebyiri z'umugoroba (7:00 AM - 6:00 PM). Kuwa Mbere irafunze.

### [FRENCH] Résumé en Français (Musée du Palais Royal à Nyanza)
Le Musée du Palais Royal est situé dans le district de Nyanza, Province du Sud, à environ 88 km de Kigali. Il offre un aperçu vivant du système monarchique rwandais et de son évolution jusqu'au début des années 1960.
- **Attractions principales**: Une réplique fidèle du palais royal traditionnel en paille, le palais moderne de style Art Déco du Roi Mutara III Rudahigwa, et le troupeau de vaches royales "Inyambo".
- **Les Inyambo**: Ces vaches emblématiques aux cornes exceptionnellement longues sont les descendantes du troupeau royal et constituent un pilier de la culture rwandaise.
- **Horaires**: Ouvert du mardi au dimanche, de 07h00 à 18h00. Fermé le lundi.
