# Rwanda Art Museum - Visitor Guide

## About the Museum

The Rwanda Art Museum is located in the former Presidential Palace in Kanombe, Kigali. It is one of the most important art museums in East Africa, displaying contemporary Rwandan art and celebrating the evolution of artistic expression in Rwanda.

**Location:** Former Presidential Palace, Kanombe, Kigali, Rwanda
**Opening Hours:** Tuesday to Sunday, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
**Closed:** Mondays
**Managed by:** Rwanda Cultural Heritage Academy (RCHA)

---

## HISTORY AND SIGNIFICANCE

The museum is housed in the former Presidential Palace, the residence of President Juvénal Habyarimana, where the presidential plane was shot down on April 6, 1994, triggering the genocide. The building has been transformed into a symbol of Rwanda's artistic renaissance and cultural rebirth.

## IMIGONGO: THE ICONIC RWANDAN ART FORM

### What is Imigongo?

Imigongo is one of Rwanda's most iconic and unique traditional art forms. It consists of geometric patterns made from cow dung that is dried, painted with natural colors (black, white, and ochre), and burned to create striking visual designs.

### The Origin of Imigongo: Prince Kakira of Gisaka

Imigongo was originated by **Prince Kakira**, son of King Kimenyi of Gisaka (present-day Eastern Province). According to historical accounts, Prince Kakira discovered the artistic technique of using cow dung to create geometric decorative patterns. He began decorating the walls of his residence and royal courts with elaborate geometric designs.

The art form spread from the Gisaka region to become one of Rwanda's most celebrated cultural expressions. The geometric patterns in Imigongo traditionally carry symbolic meanings:
- Zigzag patterns represent "two women holding hands" (unity and solidarity)
- Spiral patterns symbolize eternal continuity
- Diamond shapes represent royalty and honor

### Traditional vs Contemporary Imigongo

**Traditional Imigongo:**
- Made with cow dung mixed with ash
- Natural pigments: white (kaolin), black (charcoal), ochre (natural clay)
- Geometric patterns with cultural meaning
- Used to decorate palace walls and important buildings

**Contemporary Imigongo:**
- Artists now use modern paints alongside traditional pigments
- New color palettes while preserving traditional patterns
- Applied to canvas, panels, and decorative objects
- Exported internationally as cultural art

## WHAT TO SEE

### Gallery 1: Imigongo Heritage Collection

The most comprehensive collection of Imigongo art in Rwanda:
- Historical pieces showing the original techniques
- Works from master artists of the Gisaka region
- Evolution of the art form from the 19th century to today
- Explanations of the symbolic meaning of geometric patterns

### Gallery 2: Contemporary Rwandan Painting

Featuring leading contemporary Rwandan artists working in:
- Oil painting
- Acrylic
- Watercolor
- Mixed media

Themes include: post-genocide reconciliation, Rwandan landscape, cultural identity, and the modern Rwandan experience.

### Gallery 3: Sculpture and Three-Dimensional Art

Traditional and contemporary sculpture featuring:
- Wood carvings by master craftspeople
- Bronze and metal sculptures
- Ceramic art
- Contemporary installation art

### Gallery 4: Photography and Documentary Art

An extensive archive of documentary photography covering:
- Rwanda's cultural heritage
- Traditional ceremonies and customs
- The genocide and its aftermath
- Rwanda's reconstruction and development

### Gallery 5: The Presidential Palace History

The history of the building itself, including:
- The architecture of the former Presidential Palace
- Life at the palace during Habyarimana's presidency
- The events of April 6, 1994
- The transformation into a museum

### Gallery 6: Textile and Decorative Arts

Traditional Rwandan crafts on display:
- **Agaseke baskets**: Rwanda's famous woven baskets, symbols of peace and unity
- Traditional clothing and royal regalia
- Bead work and jewelry
- Modern textile art inspired by traditional patterns

## FEATURED ARTISTS

### Emmanuel Nkuranga

One of Rwanda's most celebrated contemporary artists, known for large-scale paintings that explore reconciliation and national identity.

### Odile Gakire Katese

Interdisciplinary artist working in visual art, theater, and performance. Her work addresses trauma, healing, and the power of art in post-genocide Rwanda.

### Gaspard Rugirangoga

Master Imigongo artist from the Eastern Province, carrying forward the original techniques of Prince Kakira's tradition.

**Location:** Kanombe, Kigali (near Kigali International Airport)
**Duration:** 1.5 – 3 hours recommended

The museum offers:
- Guided tours for schools and groups
- Art workshops (Imigongo painting classes for visitors)
- Cultural demonstrations
- Artist talks and events
