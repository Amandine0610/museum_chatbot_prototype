# Museum Ingabo - Visitor Guide Knowledge Base

## About Museum Ingabo

Museum Ingabo is Rwanda's first private museum, founded by artist-entrepreneur King Ngabo in the Rebero district of Kigali's Cultural Village. It is a contemporary African storytelling and art museum dedicated to preserving and celebrating Rwanda's cultural heritage through art.

**Location:** KK 553 st. REBERO, KIGALI CULTURAL VILLAGE, Kigali, Rwanda
**Opening Hours:** Sunday to Friday, 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM
**Contact:** +250 786 593 164
**Website:** museumingabo.org
**Founded:** 2023

## EXHIBITION 1: INZIRA Y'INZITANE - "The Story of Rwanda"

**Artist:** King NGABO
**Type:** Psychological walk-through art installation

### What is INZIRA Y'INZITANE?

INZIRA Y'INZITANE is a powerful psychological walk-through art installation that helps visitors understand Rwanda's difficult journey from 1994 to 2024 - a 30-year journey of resilience, healing, and reconstruction.

### The Meaning of the Name

"Inzira" means "journey" or "path" in Kinyarwanda, and "Inzitane" refers to the act of rebuilding or reconstructing something. Together, it represents the path of rebuilding the nation.

### What to Expect

This immersive installation guides visitors through Rwanda's history:
- The difficult period of 1994
- The aftermath and trauma
- The journey of healing and reconciliation
- Rwanda's remarkable rebirth and progress
- The resilience of the Rwandan people

### Themes Explored

- National healing and reconciliation
- Memory and identity
- Rwanda's social and political transformation
- The power of art in processing historical trauma
- Hope for the future

### Visitor Experience

Walking through INZIRA Y'INZITANE is described as a powerful emotional experience. The art pieces tell authentic Rwandan stories through visual art, creating space for reflection, understanding, and healing.

## EXHIBITION 2: KAMWE - "A Hundred Days of Death"

Visitor keywords for this exhibition: KAMWE, Kamwe exhibition, hundred days of death, 100 days, genocide memory art, installation.

**Type:** Installation Exhibition

### What is KAMWE?

KAMWE is an immersive exhibition that presents "A hundred days of death" - a profound reflection on the genocide's trauma and collective memory. The exhibition uses art to address one of the darkest chapters in Rwanda's history.

### The Concept

The exhibition explores:
- The 100 days of genocide
- The impact on individuals and communities
- Collective memory and grief
- Survival and resilience
- The importance of remembering

### What Visitors Will See

KAMWE features installations and artifacts that:
- Reflect on the trauma of the genocide period
- Honor the memory of victims
- Explore themes of loss and survival
- Encourage reflection and contemplation
- Promote understanding and healing

### Why This Exhibition Matters

KAMWE serves as a space for:
- Education about Rwanda's history
- Memorial and remembrance
- Processing collective trauma
- Promoting never-again principles
- Cultural healing through art

## EXHIBITION 3: DIFFERENT, SAME WOUNDS

**Artist:** King NGABO
**Year:** 2025
**Running:** Through April 29, 2026

### About the Exhibition

In a world marked by visible differences—skin color, race, and culture—we often forget the most fundamental truth: our shared humanity. This exhibition explores the wounds we all carry, regardless of our backgrounds.

### Themes

- Unity in diversity
- Shared human experience
- Overcoming division
- Healing collective wounds
- Global human connection

## THE FOUNDER: KING NGABO

### Who is King Ngabo?

King Ngabo is the Founder and Artistic Director of Museum Ingabo. He is a Rwandan artist and entrepreneur who established Rwanda's first private museum dedicated to African storytelling through art.

### His Vision

King Ngabo's vision was to create a space where:
- Rwandan stories could be told through art
- Cultural heritage could be preserved and celebrated
- Visitors could engage with Rwanda's history and identity
- Healing and reconciliation could be promoted through artistic expression

### His Work

King Ngabo creates art that:
- Tells authentic Rwandan stories
- Explores themes of identity, memory, and healing
- Challenges viewers to reflect on history and society
- Celebrates Rwandan creativity and resilience

## COLLECTION HIGHLIGHTS

### Traditional Weapons

Museum Ingabo houses a collection of traditional Rwandan weapons including spears, shields, and archery equipment. These artifacts represent Rwanda's martial history and the weapons used by different clans and kingdoms throughout Rwandan history.

### Ceremonial Regalia

The museum features ceremonial items including:
- Traditional royal attire
- Ceremonial staff (impala)
- Decorative items for important ceremonies
- Items used in coronations and royal gatherings

### Textiles and Weavings

- Traditional Rwandan weaving (agaseke baskets)
- Historical clothing items
- Modern interpretations of traditional patterns
- Textile art installations

### Pottery and Ceramics

Collection includes traditional Rwandan pottery:
- Household items
- Decorative ceramics
- Culturally significant vessels
- Contemporary ceramic art

### Wood Carvings

Traditional and contemporary wood carvings including:
- Decorative sculptures
- Functional items
- Artistic interpretations of cultural themes
- Modern sculptural works

### Historical Photographs and Documents

The museum preserves:
- Historical photographs of Rwanda
- Documents from different periods
- Oral history recordings
- Archival materials

### Contemporary Artworks

Museum Ingabo showcases contemporary Rwandan artists working in various mediums:
- Paintings
- Mixed media installations
- Digital art
- Sculpture

## VISITOR INFORMATION

### Planning Your Visit

**Hours:** Sunday to Friday, 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM

**Location:** Kigali Cultural Village, Rebero

### What to Expect

1. **Guided Experience:** The chatbot can guide you through each exhibition
2. **Interactive Learning:** Ask about any artifact or artwork you see

## MULTILINGUAL SUMMARY (INZU NDANGAMURAGE INGABO / MUSÉE INGABO)

### [RWANDA] Inyandiko mu Kinyarwanda (Amateka n'Ibiranga Ingoro Ingabo)
Ingoro Ingabo (Museum Ingabo) ni inzu ndangamurage y'abigenga iherereye i Rebero mu Mujyi wa Kigali. Yashinzwe n'umuhanzi King Ngabo mu mwaka wa 2023. Irimo ibihangano binyuranye bigaragaza amateka n'umuco w'u Rwanda binyuze mu buryo bw'ubugeni.
- **Icyo wasura**: Imurikagurisha rya "Inzira y'Inzitane" rigaragaza urugendo rw'u Rwanda rwo kwiyubaka, imurikagurisha rya "Kamwe" ryerekana amateka ya Jenoside, n'ibindi bihangano binyuranye.
- **Kamwe**: Imurikagurisha ryibanda ku minsi 100 ya Jenoside yakorewe Abatutsi, rigamije kwibuka no kwigira kuri ayo mateka ngo bitazongera kubaho.
- **Amasaha**: Ifunguye kuva ku Cyumweru kugeza kuwa Gatanu, saa tatu za mu gitondo kugeza saa kumi n'ebyiri z'umugoroba (9:00 AM - 6:00 PM). Kuwa Gatandatu irafunze.

### [FRENCH] Résumé en Français (Musée Ingabo à Rebero)
Le Musée Ingabo est le premier musée privé du Rwanda, fondé en 2023 par l'artiste King Ngabo à Rebero, Kigali. C'est un espace dédié à la narration africaine contemporaine à travers l'art.
- **Expositions clés**: "Inzira y'Inzitane" qui retrace le parcours de reconstruction du Rwanda, "Kamwe" qui traite de la mémoire du génocide, et diverses installations artistiques contemporaines.
- **La mission**: Utiliser l'art comme moyen de guérison, de réconciliation et de préservation du patrimoine culturel rwandais.
- **Horaires**: Ouvert du dimanche au vendredi, de 09h00 à 18h00. Fermé le samedi.
