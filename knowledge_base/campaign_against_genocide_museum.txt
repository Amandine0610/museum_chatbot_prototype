# Campaign Against Genocide Museum (CAG) - Visitor Guide

## About the Museum

The Campaign Against Genocide Museum (CAG Museum) is located inside the Parliament Building (former CND – Conseil National du Développement) in Kigali, Rwanda. It documents the 100-day military campaign led by the Rwandan Patriotic Army (RPA) to stop the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi.

**Location:** Parliament Building (former CND), Kigali, Rwanda
**Opening Hours:** Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM
**Admission:** Free of charge
**Managed by:** Rwanda Defence Force (RDF)

---

## Historical Context: What Happened at the CND in 1994?

### The Siege of the CND

The CND (Conseil National du Développement) building was the site of one of the most dramatic events of the 1994 Genocide. During the genocide, approximately 600 Rwandan Patriotic Front (RPF) soldiers were stationed at the CND under the Arusha Peace Accords as a political liaison battalion.

When the genocide began on April 7, 1994, these 600 soldiers found themselves surrounded by the Rwandan Armed Forces (FAR) and Interahamwe militia. The CND siege became a symbol of survival and resistance.

### The Rescue Mission

The RPF soldiers at the CND held their position under siege while the genocide raged in the streets outside. The RPA launched a military campaign from the northern border, fighting through the country to rescue not only their comrades at the CND, but also thousands of Tutsi civilians who were being massacred across the country.

The 100-day campaign, from April 7 to July 4, 1994, culminated in the liberation of Rwanda and the end of the genocide. The RPF/RPA rescued survivors and ended one of the worst genocides of the 20th century.

## WHAT TO SEE

### Military Equipment Gallery

The museum displays the actual military equipment and weapons used by the RPA during the campaign:
- Armored vehicles used in the liberation campaign
- Infantry weapons and gear
- Communication equipment
- Maps and operational documents from the campaign

### Photographic Archives

A comprehensive collection of photographs documenting:
- The military campaign from north to south
- Soldiers and commanders of the RPA
- The liberation of towns and cities
- Survivors rescued by RPA troops

### The CND Battle Exhibit

Dedicated exhibition about the siege of the CND building itself:
- The 600 RPA soldiers who held the CND
- Daily life under siege
- How civilians sought refuge at the CND
- The eventual breakout and liberation of Kigali

### Commanders and Heroes Gallery

Profiles of the key military figures who led the campaign against genocide:
- Major General Paul Kagame (Commander of RPA)
- Field commanders who led various units
- Soldiers who showed extraordinary bravery

### Timeline of the Campaign

A detailed chronological display covering:
- April 7, 1994: Genocide begins; RPA begins military advance
- April–June 1994: Systematic liberation of Rwanda's regions
- July 4, 1994: Liberation of Kigali; end of the genocide
- Establishment of the new government of national unity

## EDUCATIONAL CONTENT

### Why This Museum Matters

The CAG Museum serves multiple purposes:
1. **Military History**: Documents the strategic and tactical military campaign
2. **Genocide Prevention Education**: Teaches lessons to prevent future genocides
3. **National Identity**: Celebrates the courage of those who stopped the genocide
4. **International Education**: Educates visitors about the international community's failure to intervene

### The 100-Day Campaign: Key Facts

- **Duration:** 100 days (April 7 – July 4, 1994)
- **Force:** Rwandan Patriotic Army (RPA) / Rwandan Patriotic Front (RPF)
- **Starting point:** Northern Rwanda (Uganda border)
- **Operation:** Fought south through the entire country
- **Outcome:** Liberation of Rwanda; end of the genocide
- **Lives saved:** Hundreds of thousands of Tutsi civilians rescued
