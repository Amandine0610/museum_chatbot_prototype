# Environment Museum (Karongi) - Visitor Guide

## About the Museum

The Environment Museum in Karongi is Africa's only museum dedicated entirely to the natural environment. It features geological displays, a herbal garden, and exhibits on Rwanda's extraordinary biodiversity, geography, and conservation efforts.

**Location:** Karongi District, Western Province, Rwanda (near Lake Kivu)
**Opening Hours:** Tuesday to Sunday, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
**Closed:** Mondays
**Managed by:** Rwanda Cultural Heritage Academy (RCHA)

---

## WHY THE ENVIRONMENT MUSEUM IS UNIQUE

The Environment Museum holds the distinction of being the only museum of its kind on the entire African continent. Its rooftop features a living indigenous herbal garden, and its galleries explore the deep relationship between Rwandan people and their natural environment across centuries.

---

## WHAT TO SEE

### Geology Gallery

An introduction to Rwanda's remarkable geological history:
- **The Albertine Rift**: Rwanda sits on the western branch of the Great Rift Valley
- **The Virunga Volcanoes**: Five of the eight volcanoes of the Virunga chain are in Rwanda
- Rock and mineral specimens from across the country
- Rwanda's role in Africa's geological formation

### Biodiversity Gallery

Rwanda is one of the most biodiverse countries in Africa per square kilometer:
- **Mountain gorillas**: Rwanda is home to approximately half the world's mountain gorilla population
- **Golden monkeys**: Found in the Volcanoes National Park
- **Bird species**: Over 700 bird species recorded in Rwanda
- **Nyungwe Forest**: One of Africa's oldest rainforests with exceptional biodiversity

### The Rooftop Herbal Garden

A living collection of indigenous Rwandan medicinal plants:
- Over 100 species of traditional medicinal plants used in Rwanda
- Labels explaining traditional and modern medicinal uses
- Information on which traditional healers (abasizi) used each plant
- Conservation of endangered plant species

### Water and Lakes Gallery

Rwanda is known as "the land of a thousand hills" and the source of the Nile:
- **Lake Kivu**: One of Africa's Great Lakes, shared with the Democratic Republic of Congo
- **The Nile source**: Rwanda contains the most distant source of the Nile River
- Rwanda's network of rivers and lakes
- Water conservation and management in Rwanda

### Climate and Conservation Gallery

- Rwanda's climate zones from the lowlands to the volcanoes
- Rwanda's commitment to environmental protection (plastic bag ban, Umuganda clean-up days)
- Reforestation efforts and forest conservation
- Climate change and its impact on Rwanda's environment
