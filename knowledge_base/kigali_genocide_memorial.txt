# Kigali Genocide Memorial - Visitor Guide

## About the Memorial

The Kigali Genocide Memorial at Gisozi is the largest genocide memorial in Rwanda, providing a final resting place for more than 250,000 victims of the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi. It is both a place of burial and a museum dedicated to education, remembrance, and the prevention of future genocides.

**Location:** Gisozi Hill, Kigali, Rwanda
**Opening Hours:** Monday to Sunday, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM (open every day)
**Admission:** Free of charge
**Managed by:** Aegis Trust in partnership with the Rwandan government

---

## HISTORICAL BACKGROUND: THE 1994 GENOCIDE AGAINST THE TUTSI

### Overview

Between April 7 and July 4, 1994, approximately 800,000 to 1,000,000 Tutsi and moderate Hutu were killed in Rwanda in one of the worst genocides of the 20th century. The genocide was carefully planned and executed using state machinery, radio propaganda (RTLM), and organized militia groups (Interahamwe).

### How It Started

- **April 6, 1994**: President Habyarimana's plane was shot down over Kigali
- **April 7, 1994**: Within hours, systematic killings began across the country
- Roadblocks were set up; ID cards were used to identify Tutsi
- Churches, schools, and hospitals – previously places of refuge – became mass murder sites
- The genocide lasted 100 days

### International Response

The international community largely failed to intervene:
- The UN peacekeeping mission (UNAMIR) was present but had a limited mandate
- General Roméo Dallaire of UNAMIR requested permission to act but was denied
- Western nations evacuated their own citizens but left Rwandans to be killed

### End of the Genocide

The genocide ended on July 4, 1994, when the Rwandan Patriotic Front (RPF) under Paul Kagame completed the liberation of Rwanda.

## WHAT TO SEE AT THE MEMORIAL

### The Memorial Gardens

Over 250,000 genocide victims are buried at Gisozi in mass graves beneath the garden terraces. The gardens are a peaceful place of reflection and remembrance.

### Exhibition Gallery 1: The History of Rwanda

An introduction to Rwandan history, from the pre-colonial era through:
- The arrival of European colonizers (Germans and Belgians)
- How Belgian colonizers hardened ethnic divisions
- The introduction of identity cards based on ethnicity
- The political events leading up to 1994

### Exhibition Gallery 2: Dehumanization – How Genocide Was Prepared

Documents the propaganda and dehumanization process:
- Radio RTLM broadcasts calling Tutsi "inyenzi" (cockroaches)
- Newspapers promoting hatred
- The planning of genocide by Hutu Power extremists
- Distribution of weapons to civilians

### Exhibition Gallery 3: The 100 Days

A chronological, deeply moving account of what happened from April to July 1994:
- The systematic killings region by region
- Mass murder sites (churches, schools, marshes)
- Survivor testimonies
- Evidence gathered by international courts (ICTR)

### Children's Room

A deeply moving exhibition dedicated to children who were killed:
- Photographs of child victims with their names and ages
- Their favorite foods, toys, and dreams
- A reminder that children made up a large proportion of victims

### Rwanda's Recovery and Reconciliation

The final gallery looks forward, documenting:
- The Gacaca courts (community justice system)
- Rwanda's remarkable economic and social recovery
- The "Kwibuka" annual commemoration
- "Never Again" – lessons for the world

---

## ANNUAL COMMEMORATION: KWIBUKA

Every April, Rwanda holds national commemoration events called "Kwibuka" (Kinyarwanda for "Remember"):
- April 7 is the official day of national mourning
- Week-long events across the country
- The flame of remembrance is lit in Kigali

## VISITOR INFORMATION

### Important Notes for Visitors

- This is a place of mourning and reflection – please dress respectfully
- Photography is restricted in certain areas out of respect for the dead
- Counselors are available for visitors who find the experience overwhelming
- Allow at least 2 hours for a thoughtful visit

### Location and Access

**Address:** Gisozi, Kigali (KN 3 Rd)
**Duration:** 2 – 3 hours recommended
**Guided tours:** Available and highly recommended
